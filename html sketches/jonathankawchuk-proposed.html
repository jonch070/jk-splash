I make sound pieces installation & concert works and scores for screen documentary & narrative film , and I spend a lot of time in places worth listening to field recording for The Narwhal, NatGeo, and others . Much of the work lives at the edge of composition and environment — where a landscape boreal forest, arctic tundra, coastal inlet becomes the instrument. I'm based in Canada and occasionally collaborate with Walker Grimshaw, among others on larger screen projects Hunt for the Oldest DNA — National Geographic, 2024 .

About

Jonathan Kawchuk

Jonathan Kawchuk is a Canadian composer, sound artist, and field recordist whose work sits at the intersection of ecological listening, spatial sound, and screen narrative.

His sound installations and concert works explore the textures of place — drawing on extended recordings made in remote and transitional environments across Canada. His field recording practice has informed works for The Narwhal, National Geographic, and other documentary contexts.

On screen, Jonathan has composed original scores for documentary and narrative film, most recently contributing to Hunt for the Oldest DNA (2024) in collaboration with composer Walker Grimshaw.

"...a compositional practice that takes the landscape itself as a kind of score."
— placeholder, The Narwhal
